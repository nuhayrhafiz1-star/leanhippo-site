Business Systems. One connected operating system.

Not more scattered tools. Lean Hippo installs the control layer between your people, money, customers, stock, suppliers, approvals, and decisions.

The problem

Most businesses don't have systems. They have people compensating for the lack of them.

Information sits across spreadsheets, POS reports, people's heads, supplier chats, and scattered files. The business works — but only because people hold it together every day.

That is not control. That is dependence.

The architecture

Every system has four layers.

Four stacked layers, connected by signal lines. Not just automations — architecture that turns scattered work into one operating system.

Layer 1
Data Layer
Where the business stores what is happening — customers, sales, stock, suppliers, approvals, tasks, SOPs, and reports.
Layer 2
Workflow Layer
Where routine work moves through defined steps — requests, approvals, assignments, reminders, and escalations.
Layer 3
Visibility Layer
Where management sees what is happening — dashboards, alerts, summaries, and exception reports.
Layer 4
AI Intelligence Layer
Where the system answers questions, detects issues, drafts responses, and helps management decide.

Human approval & auditability

The business gets capacity. You keep control.

Automation removes drudgery, not authority. The system can remind, draft, route, detect, and summarise — critical actions stay gated behind people.

Human approval
Critical actions — payments, supplier commitments, customer-sensitive replies — wait for a person to approve.
Full auditability
Every action is logged: who requested, who approved, what happened, and when. Nothing moves blindly.
Permission levels
Review queues, permission tiers, and manual override keep authority where it belongs — with you.
